HSK 1 Grammar Points

TL;DR - Points to Note

  1. The question word ma is added to a statement to turn it into a yes/no question. If the question can't be answered with yes or no, don't use ma.
  2. The question words covered in HSK 1 are shei, shenme, shenme shihou, ji, duoshao, nage, nar/nali, and zenme. All of these are put directly into the place of the thing you're asking about.

    Examples:

    • Ta shi shei? ~ Ta shi wo gege.
    • Ni xihuan chi shenme? ~ Wo xihuan chi mifan.
    • Ni shenme shihou huilai? ~ Wo mingtian huilai.
    • Xianzai ji dian? ~ Xianzai qi dian.
    • Zhege duoshao qian? ~ Zhege yi bai kuai qian.
    • Ni he nage kafei? ~ Wo he re kafei.
    • Ni baba zai nar? ~ Wo baba zai Beijing.
    • Ni zenme qu Zhongguo? ~ Wo zuo feiji qu Zhongguo.
  3. ji is used to ask about small numbers (less than 10), dates, and times. duoshao is used to ask about numbers likely to be larger than 10, including money and room numbers.
  4. ni ji sui? is only used to ask the age of small children (about 10 years old or younger). To ask the age of someone older, instead ask ni duo da?.
  5. ne can be used to ask where something or someone is, usually when you're pretty certain the other person knows the answer.

    Example:

    ni baba ne?: Where's your dad?
  6. If you encounter a sentence with zai...ne, it's just the same as using zai on its own to indicate an action in progress.

    Example:

    wo zai chifan ne. = Wo zai chifan. = I'm eating food.
  7. If you encounter a sentence with shi...de, the construction is to emphasize the details of a past action. It's particularly used when you know the event has happened, you just want to know something more specific about it. Examples are below, but it's fairly advanced and isn't something you really need to use, just know it exists.

    Example:

    • Ni shi zenme lai de? - How did you come here?
    • Wo shi zuo huoche lai de. - I came by train.
    • Ni zuotian shi ji dian qu yiyuan de? - What time did you go to the hospital yesterday?
    • Wo shi shiyi dian qu de. - I went at 11 o'clock.
  8. he is only used in Chinese to link nouns, not verbs or adjectives. You can say wo he laogong qu zhongguo. but you won't say jintian wo kan dianshi he qu fandian. or ni gege hen shuai he gao.
